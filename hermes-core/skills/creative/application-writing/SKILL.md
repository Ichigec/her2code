# Application Writing

When the user needs a motivation letter, statement of purpose (SOP), or cover letter for an academic program, grant, scholarship, or professional opportunity.

## Trigger conditions
- "напиши мотивационное письмо", "motivation letter", "statement of purpose"
- "поступление в магистратуру", "PhD application", "cover letter for program"
- Any application document where the user needs to sell themselves to a selection committee

## Workflow (mandatory order)

### 1. Research the target FIRST
Before writing a single line, research the program/institution:
- What are their explicit criteria? (look for scoring rubrics, "критерии оценки", "что мы ищем")
- What values do they signal? (practical vs. academic, innovation vs. rigor, individual vs. team)
- What language do they use about themselves? (mirror their terminology — if they say "Agentic AI", use that, not "LLM agents")
- What are their flagship projects, partners, lab names? (name-drop precisely)
- What are the format constraints? (length, structure requirements, submission format)

### 2. Extract candidate strengths
- Read the user's resume/CV if available
- Extract measurable results with numbers (%, $, ×, headcount)
- Identify unique differentiators (unusual combinations, rare skills)
- Flag potential weaknesses to address or reframe
- Prioritize strengths that map directly to the target's stated interests

### 3. Match candidate → program
Map each strength to a program value:
- "They care about production experience → candidate has 8 years in production"
- "They have an Agentic AI track → candidate built multi-agent systems"
- "They partner with X5, Sber → candidate worked in similar enterprise environments"

### 4. Write the letter
Structure for Russian academic programs (1 A4 page, ~2500 chars):
1. **Position statement** — open with authority, not humility. "Я не начинающий специалист" beats "Я хочу развиваться".
2. **Concrete results** — numbers, technologies, companies. Not "я много работал", but "+8% конверсии".
3. **Why THIS program** — name it, show you researched it, reference specific tracks/partners/courses.
4. **What you bring** — your unique advantage. Don't repeat the resume — frame your background as an asset to the cohort.
5. **Career goal** — specific, ambitious, connected to the program's output.

Rules:
- Every sentence must carry information. Cut adjectives.
- No generic phrases ("стремлюсь к развитию", "хочу получить новые знания").
- Numbers over adjectives. "16 человек" > "большая команда".
- Address weaknesses head-on by reframing as strengths (psychology degree → psychometrics → statistical rigor).

### 5. Validate
- Character count within limit (Russian standard: chars with spaces)
- Every program-specific claim is accurate
- All numbers are from the resume (don't invent metrics)
- The letter couldn't be sent to a different program without edits (specificity test)
- No AI-isms: remove "богатый опыт", "уникальная возможность", "несомненно"

## Pitfalls
- **Writing before researching**: you'll write a generic letter that fails the specificity test. Always research first.
- **Junior voice for senior candidates**: "я хочу научиться" when the candidate has 8 years experience. Use "я хочу систематизировать" or "выйти на следующий уровень".
- **Defensive about gaps**: don't apologize for non-CS background — reframe as unique perspective. Don't mention short job stints unless asked.
- **Over-praising the program**: one sentence of genuine admiration is enough. More is filler.
- **Exceeding length**: count chars, not words. Russian letters are held to strict 2500-char limits.
